German

German also known as Deutsch is a Indo European language, mainly spoken in Western and Central Europe. It is the most native language in European Union. It is widely spoken in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Luxembourg, South Tyrol, Belgium, recognised and national language in Namibia. History

The history of the German language begins with the High German consonant shift during the Migration Period (c.300 to 600 AD), which seperated Old High German dialects form Old Saxon (old Low German). The sound shift involved a drastic change in the pronounciation of both voiced and voiceless stop consonants (b,d,g and p,k,t respectively).   • In German, the numbers 20, 30, 40, 50, 60, etc., have their own unique names, just like in English.
  • But after 20, when you add 1, 2, 3, etc., the pattern becomes easy and similar to what we saw earlier with numbers from 13 to 19.
  • You combine the word for the unit (one, two, three, etc.) with the word for the tens.

    • Let’s see this with examples:


    21 – Einundzwanzig


    This is made up of "Ein" (one) + "und" (and) + "Zwanzig" (twenty).


    22 – Zweiundzwanzig


    "Zwei" (two) + "und" (and) + "Zwanzig" (twenty) = "Two and twenty."


    33 – Dreiunddreißig


    "Drei" (three) + "und" + "Dreißig" (thirty) = "Three and thirty."

    Explanation of the Pattern:

    1. The units (Ein, Zwei, Drei, etc.) come first.
    2. Add "und" (which means "and") in the middle.
    3. Add the tens number (Zwanzig, Dreißig, Vierzig, etc.) at the end

    Oktoberfest :Oktoberfest is the world’s largest beer festival, held annually in Munich, Germany, from late September to early October. It began in 1810 as a celebration of the wedding of Crown Prince Ludwig and Princess Therese of Bavaria. Over time, it evolved into a massive cultural event featuring Bavarian beer, traditional food, music, parades, and amusement rides.

    Unique Fact: Only six Munich-based breweries are allowed to serve beer at Oktoberfest, following a 500-year-old purity law (Reinheitsgebot). The event attracts around six million visitors each year, consuming over seven million liters of beer.

    Question & Answers

    Q&A Section: Common Questions in German


    How do you ask "How are you?" in German?

    Answer: You can say "Wie geht es dir?" (informal) or "Wie geht es Ihnen?" (formal).


    How do you introduce yourself in German?

    Answer: You can say "Ich heiße [your name]" or "Mein Name ist [your name]."


    For Example = Mein Name ist Shreeya.


    How do you say "I am from [country]" in German?

    Answer: "Ich komme aus [your country]."


    For Example: Ich komme aus Deutschland


    How do you ask "Where are you from?" in German?

    Answer: You can ask "Woher kommst du?" (informal) or "Woher kommen Sie?" (formal).


    How do you say "You're welcome" in German?

    Answer: It can be said as "Bitte schön" or simply "Bitte."


    How do you ask "Do you speak English?" in German?

    Answer: You can say "Sprechen Sie Englisch?"


    How do you say "Yes" and "No" in German?

    Answer: "Yes" is "Ja" and "No" is "Nein."
